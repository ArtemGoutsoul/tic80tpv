# Music Plan 02 — "You Are Here"

*A drum & bass chiptune for the Total Perspective Vortex demo. Second iteration.*

This is a **creative/musical brief only** — mood, key, melody, and rhythm. It says
nothing about how to build it in TIC-80; that comes later. Companion references:
`docs/dnb-jungle-structure.md`, `docs/chiptune.md`, `docs/total-perspective-vortex.md`.

---

## 1. The one-line idea

**Three things at three scales, all at once:**

- **Fast, frantic drum & bass drums** — the churning near-field detail of the universe.
- **A slow, trippy, floating chiptune lead** — one small mind holding a single slow thought.
- **A heavy but sparse sub-bass** — rare, enormous notes that land like distant massive
  bodies, with long silences between them where you feel the emptiness of space.

The drums and the lead are two different senses of **time**; the huge, spaced-out bass adds a
sense of **size**. Together they *are* the theme — a **sense of proportion**, the one thing
the Vortex says sentient life can't afford. The frantic detail, the tiny slow thought, and the
immense far-off mass never share a scale, and that mismatch is the whole feeling.

Loosely inspired by the Total Perspective Vortex: it starts as a single point, zooms all the
way out to the overwhelming vastness and complexity of the cosmos, and at the very last moment
snaps back to one tiny dot marked **"you are here."**

Length: **~2 minutes 30 seconds.**

---

## 2. The shape of the journey (the whole point)

A single continuous zoom, out and then violently back in:

```
 simple  ────────────────────────────────►  vast & complex  ──►  ✷ shock ──►  singularity
 one dot     the machine wakes, stars appear      the entire cosmos at once      back to one dot
```

- **Start simple.** One small, curious voice alone in silence — a pinprick of sound. The
  "fairy cake": the tiny fragment from which the whole universe will be extrapolated.
- **Gradually zoom out.** Layer by layer the world assembles — a heartbeat, skittering
  machinery, then the full fast D&B break drops, the first huge bass note falls, and the sky
  fills with stars (shimmering arpeggios). Harmony widens from bare to spacious to dazzling.
- **Peak vastness & complexity.** Everything at maximum density and darkness — the shock of
  seeing *all of it* at once, and how impossibly small the "you are here" dot is inside it.
- **Back to the singularity.** At the very end the cosmos rushes inward, all layers collapse,
  and we're left with the opening voice again — then one final note — then the point. Silence.

The trick that ties it to the Vortex: **the tonal center never moves — only the universe
around it changes scale.** You are always *here*, at the fixed center of your own perspective;
it's everything else that swells and darkens and then vanishes.

---

## 3. Suggested key & the modal journey

**Home key: F minor.** (Chosen for a warm, deep, melancholy low end and a rich sense of
awe-tinged dread — the emotional register of the whole piece. It also puts the heavy bass in a
satisfyingly deep register.)

The **one tonic, F, holds from the first note to the last** — but the *mode* (the flavor of
the scale around that F) transforms as we zoom, and that transformation carries the story:

| Section of the journey | Scale color on F | What it feels like |
|---|---|---|
| **The singularity (open & close)** | **F minor pentatonic** (F–A♭–B♭–C–E♭) | The most elemental, "universal" scale — bare, timeless, childlike. The seed. |
| **Zooming out / first wonder** | **F Dorian** (raise the 6th to D♮) | Opens up, brightens, hopeful awe — the sky lifting. |
| **Vastness & complexity / dread** | **F Phrygian** (flat 2nd, G♭) + chromatic dissonance | Dark, vertiginous, overwhelming — the shock of scale. |
| **Collapse back** | back to **F minor pentatonic**, ending on a single **F** | Home. The dot. |

So the arc in one breath: **pentatonic seed → Dorian wonder → Phrygian dread → back to the
single F.** Same center throughout, an ever-changing cosmos around it. The heavy bass almost
always lands on that **root F** — the fixed anchor you keep falling back to, no matter how far
out the harmony drifts.

---

## 4. Tempo & the two clocks

- **Drums: fast drum & bass, ~168 BPM.** Relentless, rolling, propulsive.
- **Lead: felt about four times slower (~42), quarter-time.** Long, floating notes that drift
  over the top and refuse to hurry. This half-time/quarter-time gap is D&B's signature move
  *and* the whole thematic idea — the racing detail of the universe vs. one slow thought.
- **Bass: slower still, and irregular.** It doesn't keep a busy pulse; it drops when it *means*
  something (see §6). A single held bass note can ring for a bar or two while the drums sprint
  over it — the third, slowest clock.
- **A quiet Douglas Adams wink:** 168 = **42 × 4**. The drums sprint at 168; the dreamy lead
  ticks at exactly **42** underneath — the Answer to Life, the Universe, and Everything, hidden
  in the tempo. (Optional but delightful; costs nothing.)

---

## 5. The lead: "slow trippy chiptune"

Character notes for the floating voice (the emotional protagonist — the "you are here" mind):

- A thin, bright chip tone (a narrow pulse-wave color) — small and human against the huge
  drums and the huger bass.
- **Woozy and trippy:** slow pitch glides between notes, a wide lazy vibrato, a gentle
  "breathing" shimmer in the timbre, and long hand-made echoes trailing each phrase into the
  distance. Slightly detuned, dreamlike, never quite on the grid.
- **Sparse and unhurried.** It states a small idea and lets it hang in space while the drums
  churn beneath. It should always sound like it's moving in slow motion relative to everything
  else.

Contrast partner: **arpeggios** are the one place the melodic world moves *fast* — cascading
sparkles standing in for a sky full of stars. They bridge the lead's slow world and the drums'
fast one, and they're the sound of the zoom-out itself (more stars = busier, higher arps).

---

## 6. The bass: heavy but sparse

The bass is a **pillar, not a texture.** The rule is **weight over activity** — make each note
enormous and let there be *space* around it.

- **Heavy.** Deep, round, physically-felt sub — the floor of the whole mix. When a
  bass note lands it should feel like a large, distant mass swinging past. As the piece gets
  vast, a **growling, detuned character layer** stacks on top for menace and grit (still on the
  same rare hits — heavier, not busier).
- **Sparse.** Few notes, lots of silence. Think roughly **one big hit per bar or per two bars**
  — sometimes just a single long, decaying note held under a whole phrase — rather than a
  rolling, note-every-beat bassline. The gaps are not empty accidents; **the silence between
  bass notes is the void**, and it's doing as much work as the notes.
- **Why it fits the theme.** A rare, colossal bass note is a *distant massive body* — a star, a
  gravity well, a slow cosmic heartbeat. The emptiness around it is the sheer scale of space
  between things. Because the hits are rare, **each one becomes a landmark**: a huge bass note
  can announce the drop, punctuate the ends of phrases, and mark the final implosion.
- **It also just sounds better.** Keeping the low end sparse leaves room for the fast drums and
  the floating lead to coexist cleanly — no mud, all impact. Restraint here is what lets the
  bass hit *hard* when it does.
- **Where it sits.** Mostly rooted on the tonic **F** (the fixed anchor), moving only
  occasionally. It rides its own slow clock — indifferent to how frantic the drums get above it.

## 7. Melody development — the "You Are Here" motif

One small **seed motif** carries the entire piece and grows with the zoom, then shrinks back:

1. **Seed (intro).** A tiny 3–4 note cell — curious, tentative, almost a music-box phrase.
   Simple pentatonic. This is the fairy cake. *(A playful option: shape it as a little
   **"four-then-two"** gesture — four notes, then two — as a second 42 wink.)*
2. **First growth (zoom begins).** The cell repeats but gains a tail and starts to rise, as if
   looking up and noticing there's more out there.
3. **Bloom (the drop / vastness).** The cell blossoms into a full, drifting phrase — the same
   shape stretched and climbing upward (up = out). Now shadowed by arpeggios that harmonize it
   into wide, open chords, and grounded by the first huge bass notes.
4. **Proliferation (complexity).** The phrase fragments and multiplies — echoes and delayed
   copies of it layer against each other, it appears buried inside the fast arps, stretched
   and turned around. It grows disoriented, almost lost, tiny against the enormous texture.
5. **Buried, then a last stark cry (the shock).** At the climax the motif is nearly swallowed;
   then one bare, exposed statement of it cuts through — right before the silence.
6. **Return to the seed (singularity).** After the collapse, the motif comes back exactly as it
   began — alone, bare — then reduces to a single note: the very first note it started on. The
   circle closes.

So: **statement → expansion → bloom → fragmentation → collapse to the seed.** The melody
literally zooms out and back in with the visuals.

---

## 8. Rhythm — the fast D&B engine

- **Backbone:** classic D&B half-time feel — a big snare landing on beat 3, kick anchoring the
  downbeat and pushing just after, so the *groove* feels laid-back and heavy even though the
  detail is sprinting.
- **The roll:** swung, rolling 16th-note hats and quiet ghost-snares filling the gaps — the
  restless, propulsive "breakbeat" motion. Slightly loose/shuffled, never robotic.
- **Bass vs. drums.** The kick keeps the fast groove; the **heavy sub does NOT follow it
  note-for-note.** Let the kick handle the fast low-end *transients* and the sub handle the
  rare, huge, sustained *weight* — they share the floor without cluttering it (see §6).
- **Fills & builds:** each new section is announced by a drum fill or an accelerating snare
  roll; one big roll winds up into the climax. Big bass hits often mark the landing points.
- **The three clocks, always:** fast drums, slow lead, fast arps — and beneath them the rare,
  slowest bass. Keep the lead resolutely slow and the bass resolutely sparse no matter how busy
  the drums get; the tension between the scales should never resolve until the very end.

## 9. Section-by-section timeline (~2:30)

Approximate timings — think of them as scenes, not a strict grid.

**I. "You Are Here" — the singularity · ~0:00–0:12**
One small pulse voice alone in silence, stating the seed motif, tentative. No drums, no bass.
Pentatonic. A single point of light and sound. (Optional: the demo's very first frame could sit
on one held note = the dot.)

**II. "The machine wakes" — the zoom begins · ~0:12–0:35**
Skittering hats and the odd ghost-snare tick in — the first hint of fast machinery, not yet a
full beat. A pad opens underneath (Dorian brightening). **The first heavy bass note falls here
— one enormous, lonely hit, ringing out into silence** (the first distant mass appearing in the
void). The motif repeats and starts to reach upward. Something enormous is powering on.

**III. "The drop / vastness unfolds" — full D&B · ~0:35–1:10**
The break drops: full fast drum & bass. Now the collision is fully alive — frantic rolling
drums under the slow floating lead, and **huge sub-bass hits landing sparsely underneath, one
every bar or two, each one a landmark.** Arpeggios switch on like a field of stars. Harmony goes
wide and open (spacious, awestruck). The motif blooms into its full drifting phrase. This is the
heart of the piece: the universe opening out around the tiny listener.

**IV. "Further out / complexity" — intensify & darken · ~1:10–1:45**
More drum detail, faster rolls, fills; the **growling character bass layer joins the sub —
heavier, still sparse.** Arps climb higher and busier — dizzying, countless. Harmony slides from
Dorian wonder into Phrygian dread (that dark flat-2nd, creeping dissonance). The slow lead now
sounds strained and small against the churning immensity — the "you are here" dot nearly lost. A
tension build gathers toward the top.

**V. "Total Perspective" — the shock · ~1:45–2:08**
Maximum density and darkness: drums at their most relentless, **bass at its heaviest and most
present**, arps cascading, harmony at its most overwhelming — the entire cosmos at once. Then the
gut-punch: a hard hit and a beat of **total silence** — the moment of annihilation, the mind
seeing its own insignificance.

**VI. "You Are Here (reprise)" — back to the singularity · ~2:08–2:30**
Out of the silence, everything collapses inward — a swift downward rush, as if the whole universe
is being pulled back into a single point. **One last colossal bass note marks the implosion,**
then the drums are gone. The opening motif returns, alone and bare, then thins to one last note —
the same note it began on. A final tiny blip. The dot. Silence.

*(A wry Adams-style option for the very last sound: after the "dot," one cheeky, unbothered little
flourish — a nod to Zaphod strolling out of the Vortex completely unfazed. Use only if it doesn't
undercut the mood.)*

## 10. Texture / dynamics arc at a glance

```
density  ▁▁▂▂▄▄▆▆▇▇██████   ✷  (silence)   ▂▁·
         intro  zoom   drop   complexity  climax        reprise → dot
voices    1     2–3     full    full+        max    → 0 → 1 → 0
bass      –    ● (1 huge)  ●  ·  ●  ·   ●●  (heavy+grit)  ████   →  ●  → –
lead     bare  wonder  awe    awe→dread    dread/shock   → home
```

*(bass row: `●` = a rare huge hit, `·` = deliberate silence, `████` = at its heaviest.)*

The emotional promise: **wonder that tips into vertigo, then a hard silence, then home.**
Start on one voice, end on one voice; everything in between is the size of the universe —
measured out by a bass that speaks rarely, but enormously.
